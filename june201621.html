Why Writing

Quite simply, I like writing

Posted by Ivy Jiang on June 21, 2016

I write because it helps. I write and it needs not an audience. I write down ponderings, storylines, here and there. I save them in my GMail draft folder, so it goes with me wherever I go (provided there is internet connection). Otherwise, I write on a Word document and save it under some obscure name. I also jot down thoughts in any current small notebook I have at hand.

I write because it makes me feel better, from whatever state I was in. When I say I write, it’s not a masterpiece, literature essay or academic article that I’m writing. It’s a one-off thought, a repeated feeling, a unique emotion or a moment of frailty. It’s a moment of fire, with zeal and rich feeling. Sometimes they are words of desperate urgency, a call for change or a need of awareness. And other times, though the words are softer, more delicate and rosier, but they are still filled with the same fire, zeal and rich feeling.

I am neither poetic nor do I have a unique way with words. I am just using them in attempt to express a feeling or attitude. Other times, I use them to write a chain of arguments.

I thought in the past I only wrote things for other people to conceive. That I submitted this article for this entity or, on a smaller scale, sent this text message to this person because he or she needed to see what I am saying. But recently, after I wrote out my thoughts in the form of a letter, intending to address a particular person, I found I was saying the things I was saying for myself. I wrote them because I meant them. I truly believed in what I was writing and I wanted to share it with another person. I felt a passion that I was able to consciously recognize. Maybe it's the power of the letter form, but as I was writing this pseudo letter on the computer, I felt quite content then and there.
