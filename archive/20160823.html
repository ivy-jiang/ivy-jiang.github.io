Vaguely personal

Posted by Ivy Jiang on August 23, 2016
I didn’t write for the past few weeks not because I had nothing to say. I did have stuff to say on various things. Olympics, sports, the trajectory of one’s life, health, wants, needs, language, music, barriers. You know, stuff. I attempted to start a few posts on some topics many times. But I had gotten cold on each of these posts after writing the first few sentences. This post even, I’m not sure will make it out alive. I guess it made it if you see this.

The reason, I now conclude, that I had been hitting a wall with things, is because I didn’t know the answers to the thoughts and perspectives that I harbored, which led to me not want to write about things I did not know. We can question everything till the end of time. What is knowledge? What is knowing? I don’t know much out of all the knowledge there is to know, trivial or significant. I may not know things and subsequent answers, but as long as I am open to learning more about the world and what can constitute the experience of a human being, then, I suppose, I can cautiously express thoughts at certain moments in time, knowing full well I am subject to change. I say this because I want to stray as far as I can from being ignorant.

So I didn’t write because I thought I didn’t know much, if anything at all. Then I realize, it’s okay because I want to learn and I am learning (until the end of my consciousness). Yet it’s also okay to have a voice, a documentation of a process, a train and trail of thoughts. Therefore I write. I think therefore I am. Yeah. I use the word “yeah” too much and it probably is not even a word.

Truth be told, I am very thankful to have this floating site that I can call my blog. If it isn’t evident (by its lack of quality or simply its strange nature), I created this site mainly for my own sanity. A little piece of refuge. A peace of mind. A shout into oblivion. Anyways. I am going back to school in a little less than a week and I hope I treasure all the moments there are to be treasured (which is every moment really, good or bad). I will miss home and its people. When I boil it down, all I want to do is to facilitate their happiness. (Starting from this point, this post, by indirect workings, does contribute to that, so so far so good).

I’ll be blogging more, especially since I am going back to school. I imagine my head will be in this mindset of wanting to write about things constantly. I am excited to take my Fall 2016 courses and thoroughly get acquainted with them. I am also excited to see how the NBA season will unravel and new books I will read. I am excited to keep up with all that is happening back home. There will always be things to think about and write about.
